Dining

Kailani

Kailani is a sleek and modern dining experience in the center of Taniti. Kailani is very popular among young adults and older professionals alike. One major appeal of the restaraunt is its central location right next to the Mateki river which flows through the heart of Taniti.

Paipai

Looking for something more low-key and engulfed in Taniti's traditional culture? Paipai serves as a very popular dining experience outside of the town centers. As you head East from the town center, the jungle starts to creep up, and right on that border lies Paipai. Paipai primarily serves traditional Tanitian food, which has allowed it to enjoy a reputation from both locals and tourists alike.

Sights

Bikeka Falls

Bikeka falls serves as the transition point where the Mateki river goes from harsh rapids to a soft, calm flowing river. Bikeka falls is about 465 feet tall and 120 feet wide, which gives it a domineering presence over the dense Tanitian jungle. It is also not uncommon to spot plenty of wildlife around the falls, whether to bathe or take a drink of the fresh water.

Mount Ululoa

Of course Mount Ululoa serves as the primary stand out feature of the Tanitian islands. Chances are, as soon as you see the islands your eyes are immediately brought to the presence of Mount Ululoa. From almost every point on the island, you can see at least a little bit of the mountain. Many traditions surround the mountain as well, so it is not uncommon to see many shrines and sculptures carved into the rocky outcroppings on this scenic mountain.

Cliffs of Paenhanga

Many tourists come to Taniti for its white sand beaches, however if you travel to the other side of the mountain, the landscape looks like something out of a fantasy movie. The cliffs of Paenhanga are a scenic landmark where you can gaze down the towering cliffs of stone that were thrust from the Earth's crust millions of years ago. These cliffs serve as a fantastic view of the ocean for a romantic getaway.

Entertainment

Tanitari Jungle Zip-Line

See the jungle through the eyes of one of Taniti's native birds through the Tanitari jungle zip-line. This zip line travels 350 feet through the dense Tanitari jungle. You will start your journey high up on Mount Ululoa, and you will end your journey close by to Yellow Leaf Bay.

Vosika Island

A short boat ride from Yellow Leaf Bay is the Tanitian island of Vosika. Legends say this island in particular was blessed by Tanihalotep, as the island is surrounded by the massive Ulusoali'i Reef which serves as a breeding ground for hundreds of species of fish, many of which can only be found near the island. The island of Vosika is strictly protected by the Tanitian government, so the Ulusoali'i Reef has been properly maintained for all snorkelers and deep sea divers alike. While at the island, enjoy chartered fishing tours, snorkeling, diving and jet-ski rentals.

Taniti Brewing Company

The Taniti Brewing Company is Taniti's only microbrewery on the island. Enjoy many of Taniti's unique flavors while sitting out on our rooftop patio that gives an excellent view of the Tanitian night sky.

Buokao Night Club

In the heart of Merriton Landing, Buokao serves a one-of-a-kind clubbing experience where one can experience the energy of the crowd while enjoying discounted drinks on many special occasions. Feel the energy of the crowd in Buokao while forgetting about all of your troubles that lie at home.

Lodging

Tungai Resort

Tungai Resort serves as Taniti's one and only 4-star resort on the island. Tungai Resort has all the commodities you could ever ask for, whether it's a state of the art fitness center, pool, spa, sauna, dining, wedding venues, and even a waterpark. Tungai Resort works alongside the Tanitian government in providing the highest quality customer service on the island. Tungai Resort prides itself with its four-star rating it earned though its exceptionally high quality services.

Dresibasa Village

Dresibasa village serves as a unique experience on the island. Rather than attempting to bring the modern world to Taniti, instead, when you arrive at the village, you are transported to a completely different culture and community. Dresibasa village has been finely-crafted to be a genuine look into the lives of the native Tanitians who have lived on the island for more than a thousand years. See life through their eyes at Dresibasa village.
